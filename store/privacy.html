Privacy Policy — Abide

Last updated: 26 June 2026

Abide ("the app", "we", "us") is a journaling app for Bible study groups. This policy explains what information the app handles and how. By using Abide you agree to this policy.

Information we handle

  • Your name — the first name you enter, used to identify your posts to your group.
  • Personal journal & reflections — stored only on your device. We do not upload them.
  • Prayers — stored on your device. If you choose to share a prayer to your group, that shared copy is stored on our server.
  • Group posts — reflections, notes, and prayers you choose to share, plus "Amen" reactions, are stored on our server so your group members can see them.
  • Notification token — if you enable notifications, a device push token is stored so we can notify your group when someone posts.
  • Account — we create an anonymous account for your device (no email or password required).

How we use it

Solely to provide the app's features: showing your group's shared feed, sending the notifications you opt into, and saving your journal on your device. We do not sell your data, show ads, or use it for advertising or profiling.

Service providers

  • Supabase — stores the shared group feed and accounts. See supabase.com/privacy.
  • Expo — delivers push notifications. See expo.dev/privacy.
  • bible-api.com — supplies verse text (KJV); only the verse reference is requested.

Data retention & deletion

You can delete your own group posts in the app at any time, which removes them for everyone. Deleting a journal note or prayer that you shared also removes its shared copy. Your on-device journal is removed if you uninstall the app. To delete your account and all associated shared data, contact us at the email below and we will remove it.

Children

Abide is intended for general audiences and is not directed at children under 13.
